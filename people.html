Key People


  There were hundreds of people that have had contributions to make the Internet reach its   current stage. It is an ever-evolving process and the amount of work that has been put in   through decades has been endless. Because of limited space we have included three of the   most known figures that appear together with the Internet's history and are known as the   founding fathers of the Internet.

J. C. R. Licklider

Developed one of the first ideas that there could be a global network. Pioneer of concepts that last even to today: graphical computing, digital libraries and online banking. While serving as director of ARPA he influenced the creation for a better adept technology research, the ARPAnet.

Robert Kahn

Vint Cerf

Commonly known as one of the fathers of the Internet along with Robert Kahn with whom he also invented the TCP/IP protocols. Worked in DARPA where he was pivotal in developing the earliest Internet and security technologies. Also awarded the Awards that Robert Kahn received.
